SnapDoc: Preview Exporter — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 3 July 2026

SnapDoc is designed to protect your privacy. This policy explains what the extension does and does not do with your data.

Summary

SnapDoc does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data or document content. Everything the extension does happens locally, inside your own browser.

What the extension accesses

  • The current Google Drive preview page. When you click the SnapDoc toolbar button on a Google Drive document preview, the extension reads the page images Drive has already rendered on screen, along with the document's displayed name and page count, in order to build the file you asked to export.
  • Your export preferences. Your chosen output format, image quality, page size and orientation are saved using the browser's local extension storage so your settings persist between sessions.

What the extension does NOT do

  • It does not send your documents, images, or any other data to the developer or any third party or server.
  • It does not collect personally identifiable information, credentials, browsing history, or analytics.
  • It does not track your activity.
  • It does not modify your Google Drive files or their sharing settings.
  • It does not load or execute any remote code.

How your files are created

All exported files (PDF, Word, PowerPoint, images, ZIP) are generated on your device from the pages displayed in your browser and saved through your browser's normal download mechanism. They are never uploaded anywhere.

Data storage

The only data stored is your export-preference settings, kept locally on your device via the browser's extension storage. You can clear it at any time by removing the extension.

Permissions

  • activeTab — grants access to the current tab only at the moment you click the SnapDoc button, so it can read the open Google Drive preview. No standing access to any site is kept.
  • scripting — to inject the capture code into that tab on demand when you start an export.
  • storage — to remember your export preferences locally.

Responsible use

SnapDoc only works with content you can already view in your browser. You are responsible for ensuring your use of exported content complies with applicable copyright, confidentiality, licensing terms, and your organization's policies.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published here and the "Last updated" date revised.
